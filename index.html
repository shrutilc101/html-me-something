St. Louis Zoo

St. louis zoo is home to more than 16,000 exotic animals; many are rare and endangered. The habitats are spacious and beautiful, animals seem to be well taken care of. Visitors can go nose-to-nose with a polar bear in this state-of-the-art habitat that includes underwater views. Entry to zoo is free but certain activities inside the zoo are paid.

The Magic House

The Magic house of St. louis features hundreds of exhibits and hands-on learning activities geared towards kids of all ages, it's a favorite among locals and tourists alike. Popular permanent exhibits include a kids' construction zone, outdoor play garden, art studio, and the legendary electro-static generator. The Magic House also hosts birthday parties, camps, art programs, speaking engagements, and other special events.

City Museum

City Museum is a super fun place, infinite tunnels and hidey-holes to explore, slides everywhere.It has four floors of playground! Even for adults it is amazing, you can climb thru the tree house, discover the slides, and navigate through the outdoor rabbit warren of tubes and tunnels.Top floor has giant ferris wheel and a school bus cantilevered from top of the building.
